Client Project

Materializing Host's Motivations

Getting MADA hosts from potential to actual

Make America Dinner Again - Dinners where people socialize, humanize, depolarize our communities and country!

The Brief

From potential to actual

There are more people who register to attend MADA dinners as guests than those who register to express interest to host a MADA dinner. And even among those who express interest in hosting a dinner, many often do not follow through to actually hosting.

Data Synthesis - Typeform Responses

Gratefully, our client was able to provide us with access to their Typeform response database to gain better insight into our target users, potential MADA hosts. To get hosts from potential to actual, we sought to answer two questions.

1. What are their primary motivators?

Potential hosts are motivated to unify their communities and country, meet people and talk, and, of course, eat delicious food together!

2. What are their biggest barriers?

The most common barriers potential hosts feel they will face are issues with recruiting dinner guests, facilitate conversations, and matters regarding locations

Results and Reflections

Successful start

Usability testing of our design revealed that users were able to successfully begin the host journey and locate the information they needed to follow through.

Guest Flow

For this design sprint, we focused on potential hosts who haven’t started the host process as our target user. Our next design process would be to elaborate on the guest flow sequence.

COVID-19

Due to the impact of the novel coronavirus on social interactions, some users responded with wishes to conduct MADA dinners virtually.
